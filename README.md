# Saving Astronomy in Ukraine

Public website for the **Saving Astronomy in Ukraine** initiative: a community
effort to rebuild Ukrainian astronomy and integrate it more deeply into the
European Research Area.

🌐 Live at **[uastro.eu](https://uastro.eu)**

## About the initiative

Ukrainian astronomy has a history of more than two hundred years and is one of
the most internationally recognised branches of the country's science. Despite
the war, the community has kept its scientific, educational and international work
alive, and has set out a long-term vision for recovery: the **Recovery Plan for
Ukrainian Astronomy**.

Rather than restoring only what was lost, the plan treats recovery as a chance to
build a more modern, connected and resilient field, structured around four
directions:

- Human capital and scientific networks
- Research infrastructure
- Innovation and economic development
- Education and public engagement

The effort took shape at the "Recovery Plan for Ukrainian Astronomy" meeting at
Leiden Observatory in June 2025, and continued at the special session "Saving
astronomy in Ukraine" at the European Astronomical Society meeting (EAS 2026) in
Lausanne. It is coordinated by the Ukrainian Astronomical Association together
with colleagues and research infrastructures across Europe.

## What's on the site

- **[The Recovery Plan](https://uastro.eu/plan/)** — the full plan to read online, plus a PDF to download
- **[News](https://uastro.eu/#news)** — updates from the meetings and agreements behind the recovery effort
- **[Institutions](https://uastro.eu/institutions/)** — the main astronomical institutions of Ukraine, with an interactive map
- **Contact**, and a form to share feedback on the plan

The plan is a first version and a shared vision, not a final document. Comments,
criticism and ideas from colleagues across Europe are welcome through the
feedback form on the site.

## About this repository

A deliberately simple static website: plain HTML and one CSS file, no build tools
or framework, hosted on GitHub Pages. Editing the text is as easy as opening a
file and changing it.

Notes for maintainers — how the site is organised, and how to add news, update
the plan, change contacts and deploy — are in **[MAINTAINING.md](MAINTAINING.md)**.
